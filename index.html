</Employee Information>

Founded in 2003, the Canada Border Services Agency is a vital federal law enforcement agency that is responsible for providing border control that support public safety as well as facilitate the flow of persons and goods. The CBSA's motto, Protection, Service, Integrity, illustrates the principles and motivations guiding the agency and its workforce. A few of the key responsibilities that the CBSA handles include detaining individuals that pose a threat, interdicting illegal goods from leaving or entering Canada, collecting duties and taxes on imported goods, and removing individuals who are deemed inadmissible.

2022 CBSA Highlights

  • Facilitated the arrival of 50 million travellers.
  • Collected approximately $33 billion in duties and taxes.
  • Kept over 24,053 prohibited firearms and weapons off the streets.
  • Intercepted over 298,051 kilograms of tobacco preventing revenue evasion.
  • Seized 37,749 kg of drugs including 2,662 kg of Cocaine, 339 kg of Heroin, and 4 kg Fentanyl.

</Job Description - IT-Technician>

During my time at the Canada Border Services Agency I was placed on the cloud computing team in the travellers division and information science and technology branch. On this team the individuals were focused on maintaining, developing, and optimizing web applications on the cloud mainly using Amazon Web Services. The project I worked on for the duration of my coop is VTIS which came to life out of the need an application for boat vessels and cruises. At the time the current state of travel for cruises had no existing processing application for its manifest and the travellers were not being captured into a passage history meaning valuable data was being lost. Therefore I was placed in a team of 4 tasked to create and complete an application start-to-end that would facilitate the processing and managing of a cruises passanger and crew manifests. For this project the main aspect I was placed on was creating the API endpoints the frontend would call to fetch, put, update or delete data to and from the database. I used SQL to access and manipulate the database for the type of requests that I needed to handle which included GET, DELETE, POST, and PUT. For almost all the code I wrote I also wrote test using Jest. When I was finished with the API I also jumped onto the frontend and created interaces using HTML, CSS and React.js.
